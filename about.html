About this book

The Palace of Illusions traces Princess Panchaali's life, beginning with her birth in fire and following her spirited balancing act as a woman with five husbands who have been cheated out of their father's kingdom.

Why should anyone read The Palace of Illusions?
The book offers a feminist interpretation of the epic, shedding light on the challenges faced by women in a patriarchal society and Draupadi's struggle for autonomy and identity.

What is the significance of the title The Palace of Illusions?
"The Palace of Illusions" refers to Draupadi's experience of the world, particularly the opulent palace where she grew up. It signifies the idea that appearances can be deceptive and that the world of privilege and power can be illusory.

What is the theme of The Palace of Illusions?
The Palace of Illusions is about women's discrimination, struggles, identity, male domination, unique female perspectives, and the position of women during the period of The Mahabharata. The humiliation that went through is given as the challenges of life.
